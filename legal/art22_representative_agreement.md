## 4. Duties of the Representative (Art. 22(3) EU AI Act)

The Representative shall, for each covered AI system:

1. **Verify** that the EU Declaration of Conformity and the technical
   documentation have been drawn up, and that a conformity assessment
   procedure has been carried out, by the Provider.
2. **Keep** at the disposal of the national competent authorities and
   national authorities referred to in Article 74(10) EU AI Act, for a
   period of **10 years after the AI system has been placed on the
   market or put into service**:
   - a copy of the EU Declaration of Conformity;
   - the technical documentation referred to in Article 11 and Annex IV;
   - a description of the conformity assessment procedure carried out;
   - information about any certificate issued by a notified body.
3. **Provide**, upon reasoned request from a competent authority, all
   the information and documentation necessary to demonstrate
   conformity of the AI system with the requirements of Chapter III,
   Section 2, including access to the logs automatically generated by
   the high-risk AI system (Article 22(3)(c)).
4. **Cooperate** with competent authorities, upon reasoned request, on
   any action the authority takes to reduce and mitigate the risks
   posed by the AI system.
5. **Comply** with the registration obligations referred to in
   Article 49, or if the registration is carried out by the Provider,
   ensure that the information referred to in Annex VIII, Section A,
   point 3, is correct.
6. **Terminate** this mandate if the Representative considers or has
   reason to believe that the Provider acts contrary to its obligations
   pursuant to the EU AI Act. The Representative shall immediately
   inform the relevant market surveillance authority of any such
   termination and its grounds.

## 5. Duties of the Provider

The Provider undertakes to:

1. **Provide** to the Representative, before placing any AI system on
   the EU market, all documentation required under Article 11 and
   Annex IV (technical documentation), Article 47 (EU Declaration of
   Conformity), Article 12 (record-keeping), and any supporting
   evidence needed for conformity assessment.
2. **Maintain** a risk management system (Article 9), data governance
   (Article 10), accuracy and robustness testing records (Article 15),
   and post-market monitoring (Article 72) for each AI system placed on
   the market.
3. **Deliver** to the Representative, within **72 hours** of becoming
   aware, notice of any serious incident within the meaning of
   Article 3(49) and Article 73, together with all relevant facts.
4. **Indemnify** the Representative against any third-party claim,
   regulatory fine, loss, or cost arising from:
   - incorrect, incomplete, or misleading documentation provided by
     the Provider;
   - the Provider's failure to carry out a required conformity
     assessment;
   - the AI system's non-conformity with the EU AI Act;
   - any act or omission of the Provider that causes harm to an
     end-user or a third party in the EU;
   - any violation of a third party's intellectual property rights
     embodied in the AI system.
5. **Inform** the Representative immediately of any change in the
   Provider's legal form, ownership, registered address, or contact
   details.
6. **Appoint** an alternative representative and notify the
   Representative, at least 30 calendar days before the effective date,
   if this mandate is to be revoked.

## 6. Liability allocation

1. The Provider remains at all times the **provider** of the AI system
   within the meaning of Article 3(3) EU AI Act and retains full legal
   responsibility for the system's conformity with the Regulation.
2. The Representative's role is **strictly representational**. The
   Representative does not:
   - manufacture, train, or modify the AI system;
   - guarantee the accuracy or safety of the system's outputs;
   - perform the conformity assessment on the Provider's behalf.
3. The Representative's aggregate liability to the Provider under this
   Agreement, whether in contract, tort (including negligence), or
   otherwise, is capped at the greater of:
   - €1,000, or
   - the total Renowide platform fees received from the Provider in
     respect of Article 22 representation services during the twelve
     (12) months immediately preceding the event giving rise to
     liability.
   This cap represents a genuine pre-estimate of the Representative's
   risk given its strictly representational role, and the parties
   confirm that it is fair and reasonable in the circumstances.
4. Nothing in this clause limits a party's liability for:
   - death or personal injury caused by negligence;
   - fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
   - any liability that cannot be limited or excluded under applicable
     mandatory EU or Cypriot law.

## 7. Fees and consideration

The services provided under this Agreement are included in the
standard Renowide creator listing. No separate fee applies for:

- minimal-risk AI systems;
- limited-risk AI systems (Article 50 transparency only).

For **high-risk AI systems** under Annex III of the EU AI Act, the
Representative may charge an additional annual compliance retainer as
set out in Renowide's then-current pricing page. The retainer covers:

- proactive monitoring of national authority communications;
- support for Article 71 / Annex VIII EU database registration;
- maintenance of the 10-year documentation archive.

The Representative will notify the Provider in writing at least 60
calendar days before any retainer fee change takes effect.

## 8. Incident handling (Article 73 EU AI Act)

Upon receiving notice of a serious incident from the Provider or
through Renowide's `POST /api/v1/creator/agents/:slug/eu-incident`
endpoint, the Representative shall:

1. Log the incident in the `eu_ai_act_incidents` audit record;
2. Notify the competent national market surveillance authority
   designated by the Republic of Cyprus under Article 70 EU AI Act
   within the regulatory timeframe (up to 15 days; sooner for serious
   incidents under Article 73(4));
3. Cooperate with any investigation;
4. Inform the Provider of all authority communications within 2
   business days of receipt.

## 9. Record retention

The Representative shall retain all documentation described in §4(2)
for the regulatory minimum of **10 years** after the AI system has
been placed on the market or put into service, or for longer if
required by national law.

Documentation is stored in EU data centres (Frankfurt and Stockholm
AWS regions) and is protected by access control, encryption at rest,
and audit logging.

## 10. Data protection

The parties acknowledge that this Agreement may involve the processing
of personal data. Each party shall comply with the General Data
Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679) in relation to any
personal data processed in connection with this Agreement.

Data subjects may exercise their rights under GDPR by contacting the
Cyprus Data Protection Commissioner (`commissioner@dataprotection.gov.cy`)
or Renowide at `team@renowide.com`.

Where the Representative processes personal data on behalf of the
Provider, the standard Renowide Data Processing Addendum applies.

## 11. Term and termination

1. This Agreement enters into force on the date of electronic
   acceptance by the Provider and remains in force until terminated.
2. Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience with 60
   days' written notice.
3. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately on written
   notice if the other party:
   - commits a material breach that is not cured within 30 days of
     written notice;
   - becomes subject to insolvency, winding-up, provisional
     liquidation, or analogous proceedings under Companies Law Cap. 113
     of Cyprus, Law 34(I)/2015 on Insolvency, or any equivalent
     insolvency law applicable to that party;
   - is placed on a sanctions list referenced in Renowide's Creator
     Payout Terms.
4. On termination, the Representative shall:
   - continue to retain the documentation required by §9 until the
     statutory period has expired;
   - notify the relevant national authority of the termination if
     required by Article 22 EU AI Act.

The courts of the Nicosia District Court, Republic of Cyprus shall
have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising out of or in

jurisdictional rules in favour of consumers under applicable EU or

*Note for counsel: Cyprus contract law follows common law principles
derived from the English common law tradition. The liability cap in §6
is to be read in accordance with the test in Dunlop Pneumatic Tyre Co
v New Garage & Motor Co [1915] AC 79 as applied under Cypriot law.*
